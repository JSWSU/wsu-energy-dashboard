DIVISION 33 - UTILITIES 33 12 13.13 WATER SUPPLY BACKFLOW PREVENTION ASSEMBLIES
PART 1 - GENERAL
1.01 DESIGN REQUIREMENTS
A. General:
1. Backflow prevention shall be provided on all domestic water systems connections except for plumbing fixtures.
B. Design Considerations:
1. At the water main entry to the building, design and install two premises isolation backflow prevention assemblies in parallel, to ensure continuous operations when a backflow prevention assembly must be maintained or repaired.
2. An air gap drain for a Reduced Pressure Backflow Prevention Assembly (RPBA) shall be used and run to the sanitary sewer.
3. Backflow Protection Assemblies shall be installed in a protected equipment space, preferably the mechanical room of the facility they are serving.
4. No backflow prevention assembly shall be installed more than five feet above finish floor.
PART 2 - PRODUCTS
2.01 PRE-APPROVED MANUFACTURERS
A. Select all backflow preventions assemblies from the current list approved for use by the Washington State Department of Health, or in the current USC list of approved backflow prevention assemblies. The following manufacturers are acceptable; any other manufacturer requires approval by the WSU Project Manager and Engineering Services:
1. Febco
2. Watts
PART 3 - EXECUTION
3.01 FIELD QUALITY CONTROL
A. Assemblies shall be designed and installed in strict accordance with manufacturer's recommendations with proper clearances for testing and servicing.
