DIVISION 12 ­ FURNISHINGS 12 30 00 CASEWORK
PART 1 - GENERAL
1.01 GENERAL PROVISIONS
A. All casework shall be AWI/AWMAC Architectural Woodwork Standards Custom Grade, as established in Section 10 Casework.
B. Exceptions:
1. Storage, Custodial Room, Maintenance Mechanics Room, and/or Utility Room cabinets shall be built in conformance to Economy Grade, regardless of the overall project grade requirements, unless otherwise specified. (AWI/AWMAC - 10.1.14.10)
2. Main entries, lobbies, reception areas, and other highly visible areas shall be considered for Premium Grade applications. Confirm with the WSU Project Manager.
3. All wood laboratory casework shall meet AWI/AWMAC Custom Standard. Designer shall also consider the following:
i. Chemical resistant finishes
ii. Physical resistance
iii. Moisture resistance
C. All countertops shall be an equal grade to selected casework as specified in AWI/AWMAC Section 11.
D. All cabinets shall be factory pre-finished. After installation, Contractor shall touch-up or refinish damaged portions equal to original factory finish.
E. Casework to be installed over continuous flooring below unless noted otherwise.
PART 2 - PRODUCTS
2.01 WOOD CASEWORK
A. Certified Wood Products:
1. All glues used in production shall comply with VOC requirements with no urea formaldehyde.
2. No particleboard shall be used in any casework construction.

2.02 METAL LABORATORY CASEWORK A. All casework, work surface and service fixture construction and performance characteristics shall be in full compliance with SEFA (Scientific Equipment and Furniture Association) standards.
2.03 HARDWARE AND ACCESSORIES: A. All hardware shall meet AWI/AWMAC standards for schools and hospitals.
